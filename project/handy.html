Handy

For Master Thesis project at Kingston University I designed a social app for sign language content.

Overcome barriers and improve accessibility with sign language.

There is currently a lack of digital products that specifically address the needs of those who use sign language. While there are some resources available, such as dictionaries and translation tools, there is a need for more comprehensive and user-friendly products that can help people learn, practice, and connect with others in the sign language community.

Connect, learn, and share with others in the sign language community.

Handy provide a platform for users to connect with others who are interested in the language, share their skills and experiences, and access a wide range of resources and tools to improve their skills, all while promoting the use and appreciation of sign language and increasing accessibility for those who are deaf or hard of hearing.

Modern and empowering

Handy brands and visual identity was crafted around the concept of modernity, strenght and accessibility.
